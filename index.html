About

Who I Am

I am a Second-year Physics and Mathematics student at the University of St Andrews. My coursework spans classical and quantum mechanics, electromagnetism, special relativity, waves, oscillations, and optics. Alongside the mathematical foundations that tie them together: multivariate and vector calculus, mathematical modelling and linear algebra, and scientific computing in Python.

My real passion lies in mathematical modelling and the ways abstract structures can describe and predict real-world behaviour. I'm drawn to problems that sit at the limit between theory and computation, whether that be quantum systems, mathematical finance, or data-driven modelling and analysis. Most recently, this has taken me into computational neuroscience, where I've enjoyed working and learning across disciplines

I am trilingual in Spanish, Catalan, and English, and I've volunteered to teach English to immigrant families through the Associació Ginesta's Lucas Project.
